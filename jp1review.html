Jurassic Park Review

Gregory Parrillo | April 22, 2026

Jurassic Park is one of the most iconic films ever made. Hatched in 1993, Michael Crichton and Steven Spielberg created a masterpiece of story telling with thrills cinema to that date hadn't experienced before.

The dinosaur scenes were done with a mix of cgi and advanced animatronics. The lifelike size of many of these dinosaurs helped create the visions of what dinosaurs looked like to all those who have seen this iconic film.

While the film exaggerated the way most of these dinosaurs actually appeared based on fossil records, Jurassic Park succeeds in delivering pure entertainment.

Favorite Scene

The best scene in the film is when Tim and Lex are hiding from the Velociraptors in the kitchen. As the kids are clammoring around on the floor trying to stay as quiet as possible they can't help but manage to create just enough noise to daw the raptor's attention. It's a very intense and suspenseful scene that at the time was unmatched in its delivery.

Trivia

  • The T-Rex roar is actually a mix of animal sounds, including a baby elephant, tiger, and alligator.
  • The T-Rex animatronic malfunctioned in the rain, making it move unpredictably and look even scarier.
  • The famous water ripple scene was created by vibrating a glass of water with a guitar string.


Jurassic Park: The Lost World Review

Jurassic Park: The Lost World was the much awaited sequel to the origina masterpiece. Michael Crichton and Steven Spielberg try to bring back the same thrills that kept viewers in their movie theater deats back in '93.

The star power in this film was a little better now adding Vince Vaugn into the mix as Nick Van Owen.

This time around there isn't just one T-Rex...there are 2 plus a small adolescent T-Rex as well. You thought one was bad...

Entertainment: 9 / 10

Overall: 9 / 10

Favorite Scene

Dr. Ian Malcolm returns to the RV to find his ex wife Sarah (played by Julianne Moore) had brought in an injured baby Tyrannosaurus Rex. The baby cries and attracts 2 full grown T-Rex's to the RV. They know the baby is inside and they want their baby out.

Trivia

  • A real gymnast (Kelly’s actress) performed many of her own stunts.
  • The baby T-Rex used in the film had both animatronic and CGI versions.
  • The glass cracking trailer scene took months to design to get the physics right.


Jurassic Park III

Jurassic Park III brought audiences back in time for a third and final time as Michael Crichton wrapped up his Jurassic Park trilogy. This time however, Spielberg handed the reigns over to new director, Joe Johnston and...you could tell.

A return to Jurassic Park was always in the cards for Dr. Alan Grant. He inadvertedly found his way back to Isla Sorna to rescue Eric Kirby, the son of 2 'millionaires' Paul & Amanda Kirby. He was supposed to just circle the island...he never expcted to land.

Jurassic Park III found a new way to bring audiences to the movie theaters and this time T-Rex had to take a back seat. Spinosaurus is the new dinosaur antagonist in the film.
