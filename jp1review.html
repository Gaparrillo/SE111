Jurassic Park Review

Gregory Parrillo | April 22, 2026

Jurassic Park is one of the most iconic films ever made. Hatched in 1993, Michael Crichton and Steven Spielberg created a masterpiece of storytelling with thrills cinema had not experienced before.

The dinosaur scenes were done with a mix of CGI and advanced animatronics. The lifelike size of many of these dinosaurs helped create a vision of what dinosaurs looked like for audiences everywhere.

While the film exaggerated how dinosaurs actually appeared based on fossil records, Jurassic Park succeeds in delivering pure entertainment.

My Ratings

Story: 10 / 10

Visual Effects: 10 / 10

Entertainment: 10 / 10

Overall: 10 / 10

Favorite Scene

The best scene in the film is when Tim and Lex are hiding from the velociraptors in the kitchen. The tension and suspense make it one of the most memorable moments in the movie.

Trivia

  • The T-Rex roar is a mix of animal sounds, including a baby elephant, tiger, and alligator.
  • The animatronic T-Rex malfunctioned in the rain, making it even more terrifying.
  • The water ripple scene was created using a vibrating glass of water.


Jurassic Park: The Lost World Review

The Lost World: Jurassic Park was the highly anticipated sequel to the original.

The film introduced new characters, including Vince Vaughn as Nick Van Owen.

This time, there are multiple T-Rexes, raising the stakes.

My Ratings

Story: 8 / 10

Visual Effects: 10 / 10

Entertainment: 9 / 10

Overall: 9 / 10

Favorite Scene

The RV scene with the two T-Rexes trying to retrieve their baby is one of the most intense sequences in the film.

Trivia

  • A real gymnast performed many stunts.
  • The baby T-Rex used animatronics and CGI.
  • The glass trailer scene took months to perfect.


Jurassic Park III

Jurassic Park III brought audiences back for a third installment.

Dr. Alan Grant returns to Isla Sorna to rescue Eric Kirby.

Spinosaurus replaces the T-Rex as the main antagonist.
